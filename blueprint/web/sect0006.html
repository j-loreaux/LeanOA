Lemma
#
Proof ▶

Let

\(\)x\( be on the unit sphere and let \)p\( be the supremum of the \)p_α\(. By Theorem \ref{lem: sigma_cont_of_normal (Sak 1.7.4)} and Lemma \ref{lem: proj_of_sup_proj} we know \)p_α\( converges in \)σ\(--topology to \)p\(, and \)p\( is a projection. By Lemma \ref{lem: proj_sub_of_subproj} we know \)p-p_α\( is a projection. By the \)C^*\(--property of the norm (\)x^*x=x^2\(), Lemma \ref{lem: selfadjoint_le_norm} and the positivity of \)ϕ\( we have \)ϕ(x^*x)≤ϕ(1)\(. Now these facts together with Cauchy-Schwartz and the monotonicity of square roots, \begin{align} |\varphi (x(p-p_{\alpha }))|& \le \varphi (x^{*}x)^{1/2}\varphi (p-p_{\alpha })^{1/2}\\ \nonumber & \le \varphi (1)^{1/2}\varphi (p-p_{\alpha })^{1/2}. \end{align} Hence, by the definition of operator norm, \)ϕ(·(p-p_α))≤ϕ(1)^1/2ϕ(p-p_α)^1/2\(. The right hand side converges to 0 with \)α\( due to the normality of \)ϕ\( and therefore \)ϕ(·p_α)\( converges to \)ϕ(·p)\( in norm. Since the set of \)σ\(--continuous functionals on \)M\( is norm closed, it follows that \)σ(·p)\( is \)σ\(--continuous. We obtain a maximal \)p_0\( by Zorn's Lemma. \)

Every positive normal linear functional

\(\)ϕ\( on \)M\( is \)σ(M,M_*)\(--continuous. \)

Theorem
#
Proof ▶

The claim is obvious for the zero functional. Let

\(\)ϕ\( be a nonzero positive normal linear functional. By Lemma \ref{lem: zorn_base} we have a maximal \)p_0P(M)\( such that \)M∋x ↦ϕ(xp_0)\( is \)σ(M,M_*)\(--continuous. Assume for the purposes of finding a contradiction that \)p_0≠1\(. By Lemma \ref{lem: exists_uw_ge_normal} there is a \)σ(M,M_*)\(--continuous positive functional \)ψ\( on \)M\( such that \)ϕ(1-p_0)<ψ(1-p_0)\(. By Lemma \ref{lem: msr_th_lemma} there is a nonzero subprojection \)p≤1-p_0\( in \)M\( such that \)ϕ(q)< ψ(q)\( for every nonzero \)q≤p\( in \)M\(. Let \)xpMp\( be on the unit sphere. Then \)x^*x\( is positive and hence normal, so the \)C^*\(--subalgebra of \)pMp\( generated by \)x^*x\( and \)p\( is commutative, and is hence contained in a maximal abelian \)*\(--subalgebra \)A\( of \)pMp\(. Now \)A\( is a \)W^*\(--subalgebra of \)pMp\( by Lemma \ref{lem: wstar_of_masa} and hence is a maximal commutative \)C^*\(--subalgebra of \)pMp\(. Via the Gelfand Transform, \)A\( is star isomorphic to \)C(K)\(, where \)K\( is Stonean by Lemmas \ref{lem: cut_down_of_wstar} and \ref{lem: spec_masa_wstar_stonean (Sak 1.7.5)}. By Lemma \ref{lem: fin_lin_approx_of_stonean (Sak 1.3.1)} it follows that \)ϕ(a)≤ψ(a)\( for every \)a≥0\( in \)A\(, which holds a fortiori for \)a≥0\( in \)C^*(x^*x,p)\(. In particular, \)ϕ(px^*xp)≤ψ(px^*xp)\(. Therefore, \begin{align} |\varphi (x(p_0+p))|& \le |\varphi (xp_0)|+|\varphi (xp)|\\ \nonumber & \le |\varphi (xp_0)|+\varphi (1)^{1/2}\varphi (px^{*}xp)^{1/2}\\ \nonumber & \le |\varphi (xp_0)|+\varphi (1)^{1/2}\psi (px^{*}xp)^{1/2}. \end{align} Since \)x↦ϕ(xp_0)\( is \)σ\(--continuous, it is \)s\(-continuous by Lemma \ref{lem: bdd_sigma_cts_iff_bdd_s_cts (Sak 1.8.10)}. The seminorm \)x ↦ψ(px^*xp)^1/2\( is a defining seminorm for the \)s\(--topology on \)M\(. It follows that \)x↦ϕ(x(p_0+p))\( is \)s\(-continuous and therefore \)σ\(--continuous. This contradicts the maximality of \)p_0\(, and therefore \)p_0=1\( and the result follows. \)
